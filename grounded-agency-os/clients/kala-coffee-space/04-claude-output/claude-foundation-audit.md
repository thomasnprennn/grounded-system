## Evidence Boundary

This audit can diagnose the **clarity problem direction**, but it cannot make final visual or narrative judgments yet.

Confirmed evidence says KALA’s Instagram is active but feels visually inconsistent and unclear, and customers are unsure whether KALA is a serious coffee shop, coworking space, or casual hangout spot. 

However, the audit file also confirms that the Instagram handle, actual screenshots, bio, captions, raw customer comments, target customer, current positioning, products/services, and touchpoint references are still missing. 

So the responsible diagnosis is:

> KALA has a documented clarity signal, but the source of the inconsistency has not been proven yet.

# 01 — Visual Language

## Current State

KALA is reported as visually inconsistent, but this cannot be verified from actual visuals yet.

There are no Instagram screenshots, screenshot notes, logo files, feed references, or visual assets available in the evidence pack. The available problem is currently based on client/internal summary, not visual review. 

## Specific Observations From Evidence

* The client-stated issue is: Instagram is active, but the brand feels visually inconsistent and unclear. 
* Customers are reportedly unsure whether KALA is a serious coffee shop, coworking space, or casual hangout spot. 
* Actual Instagram screenshots have not been viewed. Screenshot notes are missing or empty. 
* Brand assets, logo file, existing style guide, and visual references are missing. 

## Why It Matters For First-Time Customer Trust

A first-time customer uses visual signals to quickly understand what kind of place KALA is.

If the feed does not clearly signal whether KALA is mainly a coffee destination, workspace, or casual hangout, people may not know when or why to choose it. That creates hesitation before they visit, ask, book, or recommend.

## Possible Root Cause

The issue may not be “bad visuals” yet.

The more responsible early root cause is:

> KALA may not have a clear visual hierarchy for what it wants to be recognized as first.

But this is not final. It needs screenshots, feed review, bio, and post examples before it can be confirmed.

## What Cannot Be Concluded Yet

* Whether the feed is actually visually inconsistent.
* Which elements cause inconsistency: photography, color, typography, layout, posting rhythm, or content type.
* Whether the logo looks weak, strong, scalable, or misaligned.
* Whether KALA needs redesign.
* Whether the visual issue comes from execution or from unclear positioning.

## Confidence Level

**Low for visual execution diagnosis.**
**Medium for visual clarity as an audit direction.**

# 02 — Narrative / Voice

## Current State

KALA’s narrative is not documented clearly enough yet.

The evidence shows customer confusion around what KALA is, but there is no Instagram bio, caption sample, offer description, target customer, or positioning statement available. 

## Specific Observations From Evidence

* Current positioning is missing. 
* Target customer is missing. 
* Instagram bio text is missing. 
* 5–10 recent captions are missing. 
* The main problem still needs confirmation: whether it is exact client wording or internal summary. 

## Why It Matters For First-Time Customer Trust

Visuals make people notice. Narrative helps people decide.

If KALA does not clearly say what it is, who it is for, and when people should come, first-time customers are forced to guess. Guessing creates friction.

The confusion between “serious coffee shop,” “coworking space,” and “casual hangout spot” suggests the brand may be communicating several roles without a clear main role.

## Possible Root Cause

The possible root cause is:

> KALA may not have a clear positioning sentence that controls how all Instagram content should speak.

This does not mean KALA must choose only one identity. It means the hierarchy is unclear.

Example of the unresolved question:

> Is KALA primarily a coffee shop with work-friendly space, a workspace with coffee, or a casual hangout that also supports working?

That hierarchy affects bio, captions, content pillars, and customer expectation.

## What Cannot Be Concluded Yet

* Whether the captions are inconsistent.
* Whether the tone is too casual, too serious, or unclear.
* Whether the bio fails to explain the offer.
* Whether customer confusion comes from words, visuals, or actual business model.
* Whether KALA’s current voice is wrong.

## Confidence Level

**Low to medium.**

The direction is clear, but the evidence is incomplete.

# 03 — Customer Perception

## Current State

There is one confirmed customer confusion signal, but no raw customer evidence yet.

The available evidence says customers are unsure whether KALA is a serious coffee shop, coworking space, or casual hangout spot. But raw comments, DMs, reviews, repeated questions, source, and date are missing. 

## Specific Observations From Evidence

* Customer confusion signal is recorded. 
* Raw customer wording is missing. 
* Repeated questions are missing. 
* The evidence index marks customer comments as partial, with low to medium confidence. 

## Why It Matters For First-Time Customer Trust

Customer confusion is a direct trust signal.

When people cannot classify a brand quickly, they do not immediately know what decision to make:

* “Should I come here for coffee?”
* “Can I work here for hours?”
* “Is this place casual enough for hanging out?”
* “Do I need to book?”
* “Is it quiet or social?”

If those questions are not answered early, the brand feels unclear even if the space itself is good.

## Possible Root Cause

The possible root cause is:

> KALA’s public-facing signals may not be giving customers one clear use-case to remember.

But this cannot be treated as final until raw customer wording is collected.

## What Cannot Be Concluded Yet

* How many customers are confused.
* Whether confusion happens on Instagram, in-store, through DM, or through reviews.
* Whether confusion is repeated or isolated.
* Whether the confusion hurts visits, bookings, purchases, or inquiries.
* Whether the issue comes from customer perception or owner interpretation.

## Confidence Level

**Medium for the existence of a confusion signal.**
**Low for the cause of that confusion.**

# 04 — System Coherence

## Current State

The brand system cannot be fully mapped yet because most touchpoint data is missing.

There is not enough evidence to compare Instagram, website, physical space, menu, signage, booking flow, pricing, or in-store experience. 

## Specific Observations From Evidence

* Website is missing. 
* Instagram handle is missing. 
* Products/services are missing. 
* Business type is not confirmed beyond the brand name. 
* Touchpoints to check besides Instagram have not been confirmed. 

## Why It Matters For First-Time Customer Trust

KALA’s problem is not just “feed looks inconsistent.”

The deeper issue may be that the brand has multiple possible meanings:

1. Coffee shop
2. Coworking space
3. Casual hangout spot

Those can coexist, but only if the system explains the relationship clearly.

Without system coherence, every touchpoint may push a different reading. Instagram might feel like lifestyle content, the name might imply workspace, the actual space might behave like a café, and customers are left to connect the dots themselves.

## Possible Root Cause

The possible root cause is:

> KALA does not yet have a documented brand hierarchy that connects offer, customer use-case, Instagram communication, and physical experience.

This is a system issue, not just a visual issue.

## What Cannot Be Concluded Yet

* Whether Instagram and physical space are misaligned.
* Whether website, menu, signage, or booking experience contradict Instagram.
* Whether KALA should lead with coffee, work, hangout, or a hybrid position.
* Whether there is a real business model conflict.
* Whether the issue is caused by lack of system, lack of assets, or lack of evidence.

## Confidence Level

**Medium for system gap as an audit direction.**
**Low for actual touchpoint misalignment.**

# Priority Diagnosis

## High Priority

### Clarify what KALA wants to be understood as first.

The key question is not “coffee shop or coworking or hangout?”

The better question is:

> When someone finds KALA for the first time, what should they understand within 5 seconds?

Until that is answered, visual and caption consistency cannot be judged cleanly.

---

## Medium Priority

### Separate customer evidence from owner interpretation.

The phrase “customers are unsure” is useful, but not enough.

The audit needs raw proof:

* exact customer wording,
* where it appeared,
* how often it happens,
* whether it comes from DM, comment, review, or in-person question.

---

## Low Priority

### Do not judge design style yet.

No conclusion should be made yet about color, typography, photography, layout, or redesign.

The evidence index explicitly says not to conclude actual visual inconsistency, visual causes, caption issues, customer perception patterns, business model, clarity score, or root cause yet. 

---

# Audit Conclusion

KALA’s current issue is not confirmed as a design problem yet.

The clearest diagnosis from the available evidence is:

> KALA has a clarity gap around how customers should categorize the brand.
> The brand may be trying to communicate coffee, work, and hangout at the same time, but the hierarchy is not yet proven or documented.

The next audit move is not redesign.

The next audit move is evidence collection:

1. Instagram handle and bio reference
2. 9–15 latest feed screenshots
3. 5–10 latest captions
4. Raw customer comments, DMs, reviews, or repeated questions
5. Client clarification on intended positioning and target customer
